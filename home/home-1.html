Market Trends and Geopolitical Factors:
Several reports suggest that heightened geopolitical tensions and economic uncertainties and increased defense spending, are expected to maintain or increase gold's appeal as a safe-haven asset. In the Arab world, nations like Saudi Arabia and the UAE are actively diversifying their economies and reducing dependence on oil revenues, a strategy that increases their investments in gold as a stabilizer.
Investment Bank Forecasts:
Various investment banks have set optimistic price targets for gold. For example, forecasts for 2025 suggest that gold could be priced between $2,800 to $3,100 per ounce depending on economic developments. Factors influencing these forecasts include central bank buying, inflation expectations, and the lower interest rate environment that reduces the opportunity cost of holding gold

Investor Strategies and Central Bank Activities:
There's a strong trend of central banks adjusting their gold reserves, and experts advise keeping gold as a core portfolio asset due to its hedge against potential financial instabilities and its historical performance during periods of economic uncertainty
Demand Dynamics:
Consumer demand, especially in key markets like China and India, remains a significant driver of gold prices. Moreover, the ongoing geopolitical and economic factors are likely to continue bolstering investor interest in gold as a diversification strategy
